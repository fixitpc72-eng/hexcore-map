Federal
One Big Beautiful Bill Act — $0 NFA Tax Stamp Now in Effect
The $200 NFA tax stamp was eliminated for suppressors, SBRs, SBSs, and AOWs effective January 1, 2026. Machine guns and destructive devices are not included. ATF Form 4 and Form 1 are still required; registration and prior-approval-before-possession requirements remain fully in place. The tax is $0, not optional.

Court
Pistol Brace Rule Vacated Nationwide — Mock v. Bondi
The DOJ dismissed its appeal of the pistol brace rule (ATF 2021R-08F) on July 17, 2025 in Mock v. Bondi, effectively vacating the rule nationwide. Braced pistols revert to the statutory NFA definition — a firearm is only an SBR if barrel under 16" or OAL under 26" AND designed to be fired from the shoulder. A brace is not a stock under current enforcement.

Post-Bruen Litigation Wave Still Active — Key Circuit Cases to Watch
The Supreme Court's Bruen (2022) and Rahimi (2024) decisions continue to drive lower court litigation over everything from magazine limits to permit requirements to "sensitive places" restrictions. Dozens of active cases in the 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 7th, and 9th Circuits will shape the practical law across the country through 2026 and beyond.

Giffords 2025 State Scorecard: Highest-Rated States Setting Template for Federal Push
Giffords' annual state law scorecard gives the highest marks to CA, NJ, NY, IL, MA, CT, MD, HI, and DC. GOA Monitoring: Universal Background Check Expansion Bills in Senate
GOA is actively tracking federal legislation introduced in the current Congress that would expand background check requirements to include private transfers. GOA's position is that any expansion creates a national firearms registry framework in practice, regardless of the statutory prohibition. GOA is running opposition campaigns and coordinating with Senate allies to block floor votes.

State
Washington SB 5215 Assault Weapons Ban — Signed and Active
Washington state enacted SB 5215, prohibiting the manufacture, sale, and transfer of assault weapons as defined by the legislation. Legal challenges are expected from FPC and SAF. Current owners grandfathered; new sales prohibited. The magazine limit (10-round) is also in effect. Washington becomes one of the most restrictive states outside the Northeast for rifle configurations.
