Quick Reference
Federal NFA Thresholds
  • Title I Rifle: Barrel ≥16" AND OAL ≥26" with stock — no NFA
  • SBR: Rifle barrel <16" OR OAL <26" with stock
  • SBS: Shotgun barrel <18" OR OAL <26" with stock
  • Pistol: No stock, designed for one-hand use
  • AOW: Pistol + vertical foregrip added
AWB Feature Bans
States restricting semi-auto rifles by features: CA, NY, NJ, MD, MA, CT, HI, IL, CO, WA, RI, DE, DC. Each state's banned feature list differs. Common banned features: pistol grip, adjustable stock, flash hider, detachable magazine on certain platforms. Verify your state's exact statute.
Magazine Limits
  • 10 rounds: CA, CO, CT, HI, MD, MA, NJ, NY, RI, DC
  • 15 rounds: IL, VT, WA
  • 17 rounds: DE
  • All other states: No federal or state limit
Suppressor Bans
Banned: CA, HI, IL, MD, MA, NJ, NY, RI, DC.
Restricted (additional state steps): CT, MN, PA.
Legal with federal Form 4 in all other states.
$0 tax stamp effective Jan 1, 2026. Form 4 still required.
Brace Rule — 2026
ATF brace rule (2021R-08F) vacated July 17, 2025 in Mock v. Bondi. Braced pistols revert to statutory definition — a firearm is only an SBR if barrel <16" OR OAL <26" AND designed to fire from the shoulder. A brace is not a stock under current enforcement.
14.5" Pinned Muzzle Device
A 14.5" barrel with a permanently pinned/welded muzzle device bringing total barrel length to ≥16" counts as a legal 16"+ barrel — no NFA required. The weld must be professional and inspected — device cannot be removable. Common legal workaround. Cost: ~$50–100 at most gunsmiths.
