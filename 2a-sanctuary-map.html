What is a 2A Sanctuary?

A Second Amendment Sanctuary is a state, county, or locality that has passed a law or resolution declaring it will not enforce gun laws perceived to violate the Second Amendment — including red flag laws, assault weapon bans, universal background check mandates, and magazine restrictions. As of 2026, nearly 1,930 counties across 40+ states have some form of 2A sanctuary declaration, making it one of the largest grassroots political movements in American history.

GOA Tier Standards
★ Gold Standard
The strongest form. Includes funding prohibitions for unconstitutional gun enforcement and criminal or civil penalties for government agents who violate citizens' rights. Carries real legal weight.
Idaho Missouri
△ Silver Standard
Strong commitment on record but lacks funding prohibitions or enforcement penalties. Demonstrates political will but offers less structural protection than Gold.

○ Limited / Symbolic
Passed legislation or gubernatorial proclamations that express support for the Second Amendment but are narrower in scope — often focused on in-state manufactured firearms or are proclamations without force of law.

Important Legal Context

Under the Tenth Amendment, states and localities can legally refuse to use their own resources to enforce federal laws — a doctrine called "anti-commandeering." This is the constitutional basis for 2A sanctuaries, the same legal principle used by immigration sanctuary cities. Federal agents can still enforce federal gun laws within sanctuary jurisdictions — state officials simply won't assist.

This means 2A sanctuaries do not exempt residents from federal law. If the ATF wants to enforce a federal regulation in a Gold Standard SAPA state, they can — local law enforcement just won't help. The practical effect is reduced enforcement of state-level gun restrictions perceived as unconstitutional.
