Guidance Notes
Shooter Ear Risk
A plain-English estimate of how punishing the setup may feel at the shooter’s position. Short barrels, supersonic ammo, semi-auto gas systems, reflective environments, and more traditional high-pressure cans usually push this bar in the wrong direction.
Bystander Comfort
This is the public-facing blast picture around the gun, not just what the shooter experiences. Host lane, environment, and suppressor type all change it. Compact cans can stay handy while still leaving a louder bystander impression than larger or more optimized options.
Gas / Blowback Pressure
This estimates how likely the setup is to feel dirty, gassy, or more abrupt on semi-auto hosts. It is especially relevant on shorter AR-pattern systems and some PCCs. Lower-backpressure designs can help, but they are still setup dependent.
Published dB Figures
When loaded, published dB values are shown as context only. They can come from different hosts, ammo types, barrel lengths, and measurement positions, so they should not be treated like an apples-to-apples universal ranking.

What This Adds
Use this page alongside the suppressor compatibility planner to understand how host type, environment, ammo choice, and suppressor style change the overall hearing-risk picture.
Why Context Matters
A suppressor that feels excellent on a bolt gun with subsonic ammo may feel much less forgiving on a short semi-auto host indoors. That does not automatically make the can bad — it means the surrounding context changed.
How The Suppressor Layer Works
The suppressor selector lets you narrow by manufacturer, model, category, status, design style, mount context, family behavior, and published dB information where available. The score bars help you compare setups more clearly and quickly.
Educational use only. Final real-world experience can vary by ammo lot, host tuning, firing cadence, mounting method, environmental reflections, and other setup variables.
