2
Understand the Federal Process

In all 42 legal states, suppressor ownership still requires federal NFA compliance — ATF Form 4 and background check. As of January 1, 2026, the $200 tax stamp fee has been eliminated, but the Form 4 process remains.

Federal Form 4 process applies in every legal state:

1.Purchase from licensed NFA dealer
2.Submit ATF eForm 4 (individual or trust)
3.ATF background check and approval
4.Take possession with approved stamp documentation

As of Jan 1, 2026: the $200 federal tax stamp fee has been eliminated. eForm 4 approvals now averaging days for most filers.

3
Check State-Specific Rules

Even in legal states, some add requirements. Minnesota requires a local law enforcement transfer permit. Connecticut and Pennsylvania ban suppressor use while hunting even though ownership is legal. Click your state for the full picture.

4
Trust vs. Individual Filing

Filing as a gun trust allows multiple designated trustees to legally use the suppressor. Trusts also simplify estate transfer — no second Form 4 when you pass it to a beneficiary. eForm 4 trust filings are now averaging just a few days. Consult a qualified NFA attorney about whether a trust is right for your situation.

What the Colors Mean
Green — Legal
Civilian suppressor ownership permitted. Standard federal NFA Form 4 is the only requirement. No additional state hoops.
Yellow — Restricted
Ownership is legal but the state adds requirements beyond federal NFA — such as a local LE transfer permit (MN) or a hunting prohibition (CT, PA).
Red — Banned
State law prohibits civilian suppressor ownership regardless of federal NFA compliance. Do not purchase or possess in these states.

Key NFA Rules

What Changed in 2026

  • $200 federal tax stamp fee eliminated as of January 1, 2026
  • ATF Form 4 and NFA registration process remains unchanged
  • eForm 4 electronic filing strongly recommended over paper
  • Wait times dramatically reduced — days for most eForm 4 submissions
  • Texas HB 957 remains in legal limbo — follow federal NFA process

Interstate Travel Rules

  • Suppressors and AOWs: no ATF Form 5320.20 required to cross state lines
  • SBRs, SBSs, and machine guns: DO require prior ATF Form 5320.20 approval
  • Never transport into a ban state — state law supersedes your Form 4
  • Keep approved paperwork with the suppressor at all times
  • When flying: declare as a firearm at TSA check-in
Key Rules to Know
🔒
Keep your Form 4 with the suppressor. ATF requires proof of lawful ownership to be accessible. Carry the approved documentation — a photo on your phone is not legally sufficient in every jurisdiction.
🎯
Hunting rules are separate from ownership rules. Connecticut and Pennsylvania allow you to own a suppressor legally but ban its use while hunting. Iowa allows hunting with suppressors but only for certain game species. Always check your state's hunting regs.
⚖
Trust filing has major estate advantages. NFA items in a gun trust can be transferred to named trustees without a new Form 4. When you pass away, the item stays in the trust — no probate, no second tax stamp. Well worth the attorney fee for most owners.
