02
Form 4 vs Form 1
Which form you need depends on how you're getting it
Most Common
ATF eForm 4 — Buy One
Used when purchasing a manufactured suppressor from an NFA dealer. The suppressor stays at the dealer until ATF approves your Form 4. You cannot take possession before approval.
  • Who files it: You and your dealer together at the FFL
  • Where: ATF eForms at eForms.atf.gov
  • Requirements: Background check, fingerprints, photo
  • Cost: $0 tax stamp as of Jan 1, 2026
  • Wait time: Days to weeks in 2026 for eForm
  • Used for: Factory-built suppressors, all calibers
Build Your Own
ATF eForm 1 — Make One
Used when building your own suppressor or manufacturing an NFA item yourself. Requires machining knowledge and the ability to engrave a serial number on the finished item.
  • Who files it: You, directly with ATF eForms
  • Requirements: Same as Form 4 + serial number engraving plan
  • Cost: $0 tax stamp as of Jan 1, 2026
  • Wait time: Generally faster than Form 4
  • Used for: Solvent trap kits, custom builds
  • Cannot build until ATF approval — not before
⚠ Do not take possession until approved. This applies to both Form 1 and Form 4. The suppressor must stay with the dealer (Form 4) or unbuilt (Form 1) until your ATF approval arrives. Possession before approval is a federal felony regardless of how long you've been waiting.
03
Trust vs Individual
How you want to register it — this decision matters long-term
Recommended for Most
NFA Gun Trust
A legal entity that owns the suppressor. Multiple "responsible persons" on the trust can legally possess and use it. Best for anyone with family, hunting partners, or estate planning concerns.
  • Shared possession: Spouse, adult children, hunting partners can all use it legally
  • Estate planning: Passes to beneficiaries without a new Form 4
  • Future flexibility: Add or remove trustees without ATF involvement
  • Remote certification: Can certify eForm 4 remotely (no in-person dealer visit required for signature)
  • More paperwork upfront — all responsible persons need background checks
  • Slightly more complex to set up than individual
Solo Shooter
Individual Filing
Registered to you personally. Simplest paperwork. Best if you are the only person who will ever use it and you have no estate planning concerns.
  • Simpler: Fewer forms, less admin
  • Currently faster: Individual eForm 4 approvals slightly faster than trust in 2026
  • Only you can legally possess it — not even your spouse
  • Transferring to a trust later requires a new Form 4 (new filing)
  • Estate transfer requires Form 5 (tax-free but still paperwork)
  • Must certify Form 4 in person at the dealer
Bottom line: If you have a spouse, family, or any chance you'll want someone else to be able to use it, get the trust. The paperwork difference is minimal. The flexibility difference is significant. Most dealers can set up a basic NFA trust for free or very low cost.
04
The Full Process
Step-by-step — from selection to range day
1
Select Your Suppressor & Find a Class III Dealer
You need an FFL with SOT (Special Occupational Taxpayer) status — commonly called a "Class III dealer" — to handle the transfer. You can buy locally or order online and have it shipped to a local Class III FFL. The suppressor ships to and stays at the dealer until your Form 4 is approved.
Online purchase tip: Sites like Silencer Shop, Silencer Central, and SilencerCo direct allow online orders that transfer to a local dealer. Dealer transfer fees typically run $50–$100.
2
Choose Trust or Individual Decide now
Make this decision before you start the paperwork. If filing as a trust, set one up with your dealer or a legal service. If filing as an individual, you'll certify in person at the dealer.
3
Submit ATF eForm 4 $0 Tax 2026
Your dealer initiates the eForm 4 at eForms.atf.gov. You provide your demographic info, photo, and fingerprints. The $200 tax payment step is eliminated as of January 1, 2026. You review, certify, and the form is submitted.
Fingerprints: Many larger NFA dealers have a Silencer Shop kiosk that scans your prints digitally. If not, use a local fingerprinting service and submit ink cards.
4
Wait for ATF Approval
ATF conducts your background check and reviews the application. As of early 2026, eForm 4 approvals are running days to a few weeks for most individual and trust submissions — dramatically faster than the 6–12 months of previous years. You'll get an email from ATF when approved.
Status check: Log into your ATF eForms account at eForms.atf.gov to monitor your application status. Your dealer can also check on your behalf.
5
Pick Up & Keep Your Paperwork
Once approved, ATF emails the dealer your approved Form 4. You complete the final transfer paperwork and take your suppressor home. Keep your approved Form 4 accessible whenever you use the suppressor. When traveling across state lines, you do not need ATF Form 5320.20 for suppressors (only SBRs, SBSs, and machine guns require that form).
05
Caliber Selection Guide
Match your suppressor to how you actually shoot
Key principle: A larger-caliber suppressor can run smaller calibers, but not the reverse. A .30 cal suppressor works on 5.56, .308, and .300 BLK. A dedicated 9mm pistol can cannot run on a rifle. When in doubt, go one caliber up for versatility.
.22 LR / Rimfire
Pistols, rifles, bolt-action
Best entry point. Rimfire suppressors are often the cheapest and easiest to shoot suppressed. Many are user-serviceable for cleaning. Cannot be used on centerfire calibers.
Best Entry Point
9mm / Pistol
Handguns, PCCs, MP5-style
Most popular pistol can. Works on 9mm, .45 ACP, .40 S&W (with adapter). Heavier than rimfire but extremely effective. Look for direct-thread or Nielsen device for semi-auto pistols.
Most Popular Pistol
5.56 / .223
AR-15, M4-style rifles
Dedicated 5.56 cans are lighter and more compact. Note: 5.56 is supersonic — you'll still hear a supersonic crack. For truly quiet, use subsonic .300 BLK with a .30 cal can.
AR-15 Standard
.30 Cal / .308
AR-10, bolt rifles, .300 BLK
Most versatile option. A .30 cal can works on 5.56, 6.5 Creedmoor, .308, and .300 BLK. Best single suppressor if you shoot multiple platforms. Heavier but worth the flexibility.
Best Versatility
.300 BLK Subsonic
AR-15 with .300 BLK upper
The quietest commonly available suppressed combination. Subsonic .300 BLK through a .30 cal can on a short barrel is hearing-safe without ear protection. Popular for home defense and hunting.
Quietest Setup
Multi-Caliber
Multiple platforms
Some suppressors (like the Dead Air Nomad) are rated for multiple calibers out of the box. Larger, heavier, and more expensive — but one can covers your entire safe. Good for collectors or mixed fleets.
