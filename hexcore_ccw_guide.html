Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all states require a permit?

No. 28 states now allow permitless (constitutional) carry for legal residents, but permits are still valuable for reciprocity travel to states that require them.

Can I carry as soon as I apply?

No. Do not carry concealed until you have met your state's legal requirements and, where required, received your permit. Processing times vary from 14 days to 6+ months.

Is training always required?

No. Training requirements vary significantly. Some states require 0 hours, others require 16 hours of classroom and live-fire training. Quality instruction is always recommended.

Which non-resident permits are most recognized?

Florida and Utah non-resident permits together cover nearly all states that recognize any out-of-state permit. FL: honored in 37 states. UT: honored in 36 states.

What is constitutional carry?

Constitutional carry (permitless carry) allows legal gun owners to carry concealed (and often openly) without obtaining a state permit. As of 2025, 28 states allow some form of permitless carry.

What is the duty to inform?

Some states require you to IMMEDIATELY tell a police officer you are carrying a firearm when stopped. Failure to do so can be a criminal offense. Always know your state's requirement.

Are 30.06 and 30.07 signs real laws?

In Texas, 30.06 (no concealed carry) and 30.07 (no open carry) signs have the force of law. Ignoring them is a criminal offense. Many other states also give posted signs legal weight.

Why use official sources?

Laws change — sometimes rapidly. A court ruling, new legislation, or AG opinion can change the rules without widespread news coverage. Always verify with the official issuing authority before carrying.
