Alabama
Concealed Carry
Laws

Alabama is a permitless carry state for qualifying adults, but the state still keeps its county-issued concealed carry permit system in place for travelers who want permit documentation for reciprocity and trip planning.

This page is built as a clean Alabama overview for responsible gun owners who want the fast answer first, then the state-specific details that matter most before they carry.

Alabama At A Glance
01
Permitless Carry
Qualifying adults generally do not need a permit to carry a pistol concealed or in a vehicle in Alabama. That change took effect on January 1, 2023.
02
Permits Still Matter
Alabama residents can still apply through their county sheriff for a concealed carry permit, which remains useful for reciprocity when traveling out of state.
03
Restricted Places Still Apply
Permitless carry does not erase building restrictions, posted limited-access facilities, or property-owner rights. Those details still matter before you walk in.
Core Alabama Carry Rules
01
Concealed Carry

Alabama no longer requires a permit for most lawful adults to carry a pistol concealed or in a vehicle, but prohibited-person rules and location restrictions still control who can carry and where.

02
Disclosure To Law Enforcement

If an officer acting in the line of duty asks whether you are armed, Alabama law requires a truthful answer. That is the key disclosure rule most carriers should remember.

03
Private Property Still Counts

Property owners can still restrict or prohibit firearms on their premises. Alabama's rules also continue to treat posted restrictions and notice from the property owner seriously.

Practical Carry Notes
Open Carry
Open carry is generally lawful in Alabama, but private-property consent and specific restricted-place rules can still change the answer depending on where you are standing.
Vehicle Carry
Permitless carry covers most lawful adult vehicle possession in Alabama. The old permit requirement for a pistol in a vehicle was repealed with the 2022 law change.
State Parks
Alabama State Parks allow handguns for lawful personal protection, provided they are not used for any unlawful purpose and any park-specific prohibited areas are respected.
Restaurants And Hotels
Alabama does not impose the same kind of broad restaurant or hotel carry ban some states use, but posted rules, private-property restrictions, and other state or federal limits can still control.
Training
Alabama does not require a training class to carry or to apply for a county permit, but that does not change the need to understand safe handling, storage, and use-of-force law.
Nonresident Permits
Alabama does not issue concealed carry permits to out-of-state residents. Residents apply through the sheriff in the county where they live.
Places To Double-Check Before You Carry

Alabama law still lists several places and building types where carry restrictions remain active. This is where people get sloppy if they only remember the words permitless carry and stop reading there.

  • Law enforcement and detention facilities including police, sheriff, highway patrol, jail, prison, and similar secure custody locations.
  • Courthouses and certain government meeting spaces including courthouses, courthouse annexes, some district attorney buildings, and some active county commission or city council meetings.
  • Psychiatric inpatient facilities where Alabama law specifically restricts firearm possession inside the facility.
  • Athletic event facilities especially school-sponsored events and professional events not related to firearms, where the statute creates specific limits and permit-based exceptions in some settings.
  • Posted limited-access buildings where access is controlled by guards, screening, badge systems, turnstiles, or similar security features.

HexCore takeaway: Alabama is friendlier than many states on general carry, but it is still not a state where you should treat every building the same. Restricted facilities, posted limited-access buildings, and private-property notice rules are where responsible carriers need to slow down and verify the details.

Permit Snapshot

Even though Alabama allows permitless carry, the permit system still matters for residents who travel or want reciprocity documentation. County sheriffs issue permits to Alabama residents, and Alabama law still supports multi-year permit options and reciprocity recognition for qualifying permit holders.

Issuing Authority
Your county sheriff.
Permit Availability
Available to Alabama residents even after permitless carry took effect.
Training Requirement
No general Alabama training requirement for permit issuance.
Term
County permits are commonly issued in 1-year or 5-year terms, and Alabama law also allows lifetime permits in qualifying cases.
